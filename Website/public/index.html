Sign Language

American Sign Language (ASL) is a complete, natural language used by the Deaf community in the United States and

English-speaking countries. With its own grammar syntax, ASL combines hand shapes,

facial expressions and body movements to create meaningful communication.

About Sign Language

Sign languages are complete languages, not just hand signals. They're used by Deaf communities and have their own grammar, vocabulary and structure, just like spoken languages. Think of them as languages that use visual communication instead of sound. They're diverse, with different sign languages existing around the world.

Why Learn ASL?

  • Enhance communication with Deaf individuals
  • Improve cognitive abilities and spatial awareness
  • Expand career opportunities in education and healthcare
